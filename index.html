The Challenge

The Statistical Trap

Neuroscientists have studied these two layers separately. When trying to compare brain images, they often found false connections or inflated false positives. Why? Because the brain is a smooth map where neighboring vertices are dependent of each other, not independent points. This is called Spatial Autocorrelation.

üß†

The Spin Test

We use the Alexander Bloch Spin Test. Instead of shuffling data randomly (destorying the brain map's structure), we project the data onto a sphere and rotate it.

This preserves the spatial topology of the brain map while breaking and randomizing the alignment between brain maps. This ensures that any correlation we find is real and not just a coincidence.
